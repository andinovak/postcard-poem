poem: |
  Specks of white
  neither frost nor snow
  are first to claim
  the sparse hedgerows 

  Blackthorns stir
  as the days get longer
  buds pop open 
  as the sun gets stronger

  Blossoms appear 
  as a dusting of snow
  cheering spring on 
  in the ancient hedgerows.
